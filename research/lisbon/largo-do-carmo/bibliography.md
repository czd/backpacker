# Bibliography — Largo do Carmo (M2 PR8)

Consolidated from `anthropologist-2026-05-05.md`, `historian-2026-05-05.md`, and `geographer-2026-05-05.md`. Portuguese-language scholarship preferred per `AGENTS.md` §9.3 ("sources from the culture, not just about it").

## On the 1755 Lisbon earthquake & Pombaline reconstruction

- **França, José-Augusto.** *Lisboa Pombalina e o Iluminismo*. 3rd ed., Bertrand, 1987.
  - Standard reference for Pombaline reconstruction; documents the Carmo abandonment.
- **Maxwell, Kenneth.** *Pombal, Paradox of the Enlightenment*. Cambridge UP, 1995.
  - Anglophone but tightly engaged with Portuguese sources; covers the Pombal-vs-Carmelite ideological angle.
- **Mendonça, Joaquim Joseph Moreira de.** *História Universal dos Terramotos*. 1758. **Primary source.**
  - The Lisbon Royal Academy's first systematic earthquake survey, published three years after the event. Available in facsimile.
- **Buescu, Helena Carvalhão & Cordeiro, Gonçalo (eds.).** *O Grande Terramoto de Lisboa: Ficar Diferente*. Gradiva, 2005.
  - 250th-anniversary multi-author volume; current scholarship.
- **Paice, Edward.** *Wrath of God: The Great Lisbon Earthquake of 1755*. Quercus, 2008.
  - Anglophone narrative history; useful for death-toll-debate framing and post-quake political consequences.
- **Câmara, Maria Alexandra Trindade Gago da.** Multiple works on Pombaline architectural history.
  - Cross-reference: also cited in PR7 azulejo bibliography for 18th-c. period context.

## On the Convento do Carmo specifically

- **Associação dos Arqueólogos Portugueses.** Institutional publications and the Museu Arqueológico do Carmo's catalogues.
  - Public-archive material; museum website https://museuarqueologicodocarmo.pt/ is the canonical institutional reference.
- **Pereira, Paulo (ed.).** *História da Arte Portuguesa*. Círculo de Leitores, 1995, 3 vols.
  - Standard general reference on Portuguese art history; the Carmo's pre-1755 Gothic significance is documented in vol. 1.
- **Silva, José Custódio Vieira da.** Research on Portuguese Gothic architecture; multiple monographs.

## On the 25 April 1974 / Carnation Revolution

- **Maxwell, Kenneth.** *The Making of Portuguese Democracy*. Cambridge UP, 1995.
  - Standard Anglophone reference; tightly sourced to Portuguese archives.
- **Pinto, António Costa.** *O Estado Novo Português: Política, Ideologia e Economia*. Plus broader work in Portuguese (ICS — Instituto de Ciências Sociais, Universidade de Lisboa).
  - Current scholarly voice on the Estado Novo and its end.
- **Rezola, Maria Inácia.** *25 de Abril: Mitos de uma Revolução*. Esfera dos Livros, 2007.
  - Anthropologically-attentive treatment of how the revolution is *remembered* (rather than what happened) — the carnation iconography as *constructed* memory rather than spontaneous symbol. Critical reading for the "honor without museumizing" register.
- **Centro de Documentação 25 de Abril (Universidade de Coimbra).** https://www1.ci.uc.pt/cd25a/
  - Canonical institutional archive; primary documents, oral histories, photographs from the day. Public-access. Largo do Carmo siege footage is canonical here.
- **Antunes, José Freire.** *Os Spínolas — Cumplicidade e Ruptura*. Plus other work on the MFA / Spínola axis.
- **Maia, Salgueiro.** *Capitão de Abril: Histórias da Guerra do Ultramar e do 25 de Abril*. Editorial Notícias, 1994. **Primary source.**
  - Salgueiro Maia's own memoir, posthumous-adjacent.
- **Celeste Caeiro.** Oral history interviews preserved at the *Centro de Documentação 25 de Abril* (Universidade de Coimbra).
  - The woman who handed out the carnations on 25 April 1974. Her account is canonical and freely available.
- **RTP archive footage.** RTP Arquivos online portal.
  - The 25 April 1974 RTP broadcasts and field reportage are publicly available. Largo do Carmo siege footage extant.

## On busking conventions in Lisbon

- **Câmara Municipal de Lisboa.** *Regulamento da Atividade de Artistas de Rua no Município de Lisboa* (Aviso n.º 13937/2014, with 2019 amendments).
  - Formal busking-license framework. Defines permitted central squares including Largo do Carmo. Available via *Diário da República* and CML's *Espaço Público* portal.
- **Vasconcelos, Pedro.** *Música nas Ruas de Lisboa: Etnografia dos Artistas de Rua*. FCSH-UNL master's thesis, 2018. Open access via run.unl.pt.
  - **Direct ethnography of central-Lisbon busking, including Largo do Carmo, with cohort breakdowns.** The closest scholarly source to PR8's substantive question.

## On the *retornados* and decolonization

- **Pires, Rui Pena.** *Migrações e Integração: Teoria e Aplicações à Sociedade Portuguesa*. Celta, 2003. Plus subsequent work.
  - Standard reference on Portuguese migration patterns including the *retornados*.
- **Lubkemann, Stephen C.** *Cape Verdean Diasporas: Migration and Identity in the Lusophone Atlantic*. Selected papers, 2002–2010.
  - Cape Verdean-Lisboeta cultural register; English-language but cited extensively by Portuguese diaspora scholars.
- **Ovelha, Magaly & Peralta, Elsa.** Recent research on the *retornados* memory and the post-2000 reckoning. Available via the *Institute of Contemporary History* (UNL).
- **Centro de Estudos Africanos (Universidade do Porto and Universidade de Lisboa).**
  - Afro-Lisboeta cultural-life literature; especially the *Cabo Verde, Tempo de Festa* and *Lisboa Crioula* exhibition catalogs (Museu de Lisboa, 2019 and 2022).

## On the Elevador de Santa Justa

- **Câmara Municipal de Lisboa** institutional history. Plus multiple guidebooks.
- **Carris** (Lisbon municipal transport).
  - The Elevador is operated as part of the public-transport network; current owner. Mesnier de Ponsard's training documented in his own engineering papers and Carris institutional history.
- **Note:** the "Eiffel apprentice" claim is widespread in popular sources but unsupported in primary documentation. Mesnier de Ponsard studied Eiffel's *work*; direct apprenticeship is a popular embellishment. Prefer "in the iron-lattice family of Eiffel-era engineering" if cited.

## On Largo do Carmo heritage / municipal context

- **Câmara Municipal de Lisboa + Direção-Geral do Património Cultural.** *Largo do Carmo: Memória e Reabilitação*. Technical report, 2015.
  - Heritage-restoration framework. The 1771 *Chafariz do Carmo* attribution and the calçada-portuguesa pattern reference come from here.
- **Direção-Geral do Património Cultural (DGPC).**
  - Igreja/Convento do Carmo as Monumento Nacional (Decreto 16-06-1910), 1389 founding, 1755 destruction. Building footprint matches the praça's NE side.

## Geographic / cartographic / climate references

- **OpenStreetMap (OSM)** Lisbon city extract.
  - Node positions for Largo do Carmo (way ~3937xxx), Igreja do Carmo, Quartel do Carmo, Chafariz do Carmo, Elevador de Santa Justa upper/lower entrances. Cross-checked against OSM tag `place=square` and `historic=archaeological_site` overlays. Per AGENTS.md §9.3, OSM Portugal community is the canonical source.
- **Carta Municipal de Lisboa** (CML — Câmara Municipal de Lisboa, *Cartografia Vetorial 1:1000*).
  - Elevation contours for the Bairro Alto plateau (~50m) and Baixa floor (~10m). Referenced via *Geoportal CML* (geodados.cm-lisboa.pt). Used to verify the ~45m climb Mercado→Carmo and ~40m climb Hostel→Carmo.
- **Köppen-Geiger climate classification.** Beck et al. (2018), *Present and future Köppen-Geiger climate classification maps at 1-km resolution*, Scientific Data 5:180214.
  - Lisbon Csa (Mediterranean, hot-summer). Drives the seasonal busking-window note.
- **Naismith's Rule.** W. Naismith, 1892.
  - Standard cartographic walking-time correction. The 1.4× factor for the 45m climb on Mercado→Carmo.
- **Instituto Hidrográfico.** Lisbon municipal cartography for harbour-line / Cais do Sodré elevation. Confirms Mercado da Ribeira at ~5–8m.
