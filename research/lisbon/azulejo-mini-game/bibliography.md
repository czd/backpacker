# Bibliography — azulejo mini-game (M2 PR7)

Consolidated from `anthropologist-2026-05-03.md` and `historian-2026-05-03.md`. Portuguese-language scholarship preferred per `AGENTS.md` §9.3 ("sources from the culture, not just about it").

## Foundational period surveys

- **Santos Simões, João Miguel dos.** *Azulejaria em Portugal nos Séculos XV e XVI*. Fundação Calouste Gulbenkian, 1969.
- **Santos Simões, João Miguel dos.** *Azulejaria em Portugal no Século XVII*. Fundação Calouste Gulbenkian, 1971.
- **Santos Simões, João Miguel dos.** *Azulejaria em Portugal nos Séculos XVII e XVIII*. Fundação Calouste Gulbenkian, 1971; reprinted 2010.
  - Foundational corpus. Still the standard reference for *tapete* and pre-1700 work; chapter on *padrões de tapete* covers exactly the polychrome pattern family the mini-game uses.
- **Meco, José.** *O Azulejo em Portugal*. Alfa, 1989 (revised editions through 2000s).
  - Standard general survey, extensively illustrated. Updates Santos Simões with later scholarship and includes attribution debates.
- **Câmara, Maria Alexandra Trindade Gago da.** *Azulejaria do Século XVIII em Portugal*.
  - Standard reference for the 18th-c. period.

## Master Cycle / attribution scholarship

- **Pais, Alexandre Nobre.** Multiple essays in *ARTIS — Revista de História da Arte*; MNAz curator. Current scholarly voice on Master Cycle attribution.
- **Salema de Carvalho, Rosário.** *A Pintura do Azulejo em Portugal [1675–1725]*. PhD thesis, FLUL, 2012. Open access via repositorio.ul.pt.
  - Studies the polychrome-to-blue-and-white transition at scholar level; the figurative panels she catalogues are the right register for the 18th-c. blue-and-white panel.
- **Mangucci, Celso.** Research on the Bernardes workshop and Master Cycle attribution; *Cadernos do Arquivo Municipal* and *ARTIS*.
- **Henriques, Paulo (ed.).** Exhibition catalogues from MNAz, especially the *O Azulejo Português* series.
- **Arruda, Luísa & Coutinho, Maria João Pereira.** Research on tile production geography in 17th-c. Lisbon, *ARTIS*.

## Materials / conservation science

- **Mimoso, João Manuel** (LNEC — Laboratório Nacional de Engenharia Civil).
  - Technical scientific work on azulejo materials degradation, salt damage, and restoration methodology.
  - Multiple papers in *Studies in Conservation* and *Journal of Cultural Heritage*; LNEC technical reports available in Portuguese.
  - The reference for the material-reality questions in Topic A of the Historian's report.

## Institutional / urban-policy context

- **Câmara Municipal de Lisboa — *Programa AZULEJO* documentation** (publicly available CML publications, c. 2013).
- **Banco do Azulejo** — joint CML / Museu Nacional do Azulejo inventory of recovered/donated tiles available to property owners restoring historical buildings.
- **SRU Lisboa** — *Sociedade de Reabilitação Urbana* annual reports.
- **GTH (Gabinete Técnico de Habitação)** — historical documentation. Both available via Câmara Municipal de Lisboa archives.
- **Polícia Judiciária / Direção-Geral do Património Cultural** — publications on the c. 2010–2015 azulejo-theft wave and the resulting recovery programs.

## Digital references / image databases

- **Az Infinitum** — `https://redeazulejo.fcsh.unl.pt/` (also `azinfinitum.uls.pt`). Sistema de Referência e Indexação de Azulejo. The contemporary research network *Az — Rede de Investigação em Azulejo* (ARTIS / Universidade de Lisboa) runs the database. Canonical attribution and inventory tool with thousands of catalogued panels by typology. Use for actual panel imagery (with appropriate license attribution per §9.3).
- **MatrizNet** — `matriznet.dgpc.pt`. Direção-Geral do Património Cultural national heritage portal; MNAz collection items catalogued with high-resolution images.

## Reference institution

- **Museu Nacional do Azulejo** — Convento da Madre de Deus, Lisbon. Founded 1965; designated *Museu Nacional* 1980. Permanent collection organized chronologically, walking the polychrome → blue-and-white → polychrome arc. The mini-game's narrative anchor: the master "trained there in the 90s" but is not employed by the museum.
